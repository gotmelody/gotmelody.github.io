Welcome!

I'm a 6th year PhD candidate in Mathematics at UC Santa Barbara. My advisor is Stephen Bigelow. I received my Mathematics B.S. from UC Davis in 2014 and my Mathematics M.A. from the University of Oklahoma in 2017.

Starting in Fall 2025, I will be a Ross Assistant Professor at The Ohio State University! I will be working with David Penneys, Micah Chrisman, and Thomas Kerler.

CV

My research interests are broadly in quantum algebra, operator algebras, and low-dimensional topology. My thesis is on subfactor planar algebras, which is a subject with connections to a variety of mathematics including tensor categories, knot theory, subfactors, mathematical physics, and representation theory.

I particularly like topics related to the following:

  • Diagrammatic algebras; skein theories; planar algebras
  • Higher categories; 2-categories; fusion categories
  • Knots and their invariants; TQFTs; topological quantum computing
  • Subfactors and their classification; standard invariants of subfactors
  • Quantum symmetry; quantum groups; Hopf algebras
  • Classification and structure of C*-algebras; nuclear dimension of C*-algebras
  • Skein algebras; skein modules; cluster algebras
